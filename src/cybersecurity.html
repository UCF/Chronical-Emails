With a history of national success in cyberdefense competitions and advancements in cybsersecurity research and academia, Yan Solihin knew UCF was where he needed to be after serving as director for the U.S. National Science Foundation’s Secure and Trustworthy Cyberspace program. With more than 2 years of experience at the NSF, Solihin had a chance to see the spectrum of cybersecurity research across the United States and he easily recognized UCF as a flourishing innovative center that could help fill the nation’s talent shortage.
As director of UCF’s Cyber Security and Privacy cluster, the computer science professor knows first-hand the university’s commitment to invest in cybersecurity research, education, training, talent development, and collaborations with tech companies. From Solihin’s first visit to the campus he saw the many defense companies and government entities just around the corner from UCF’s computer science labs — including the Lockheed Martin Cyber Innovation Lab that opened in 2019 — and the Institute for Modeling and Simulation. With UCF graduates working in cybersecurity for giants like Raytheon, Boeing, Google and Amazon, faculty like Solihin — who recently helped launch UCF’s master’s in cybersecurity program, have an opportunity to increase the talent pool needed to secure the nation.

The best universities unleash the potential of people and ideas, generating solutions to society's most significant challenges. At the University of Central Florida (UCF), there is limitless potential in the excellence of our students, faculty, research and creative works and strategic partnerships that rise to today's demands and tomorrow's needs.
You recently received a packet in the mail about UCF's impressive outcomes in student success, graduate education, research and industry partnerships (including a sample of our Martian soil simulant).
Here are five more reasons why UCF is one of the nation's leading future-focused universities:
UCF is redefining what it means to be an institution powering student success. We rank among the top 25 public universities in U.S. News & Report's consolidated outcomes rankings. In fact, out outcomes are equal to the University of Washington, Taxes A&M, Ohio State and Virginia Tech — all top-50 ranked institutions by U.S. News & World Report.
UCF faculty are innovators, investors and leaders whose global and national achievements and groundbreaking research and creative works define them as experts in their fields shaping the next generation of talent. And because UCF is America’s Space University, our faculty are leaders in space exploration and probing the cosmos' greatest mysteries.
One of the world’s leading tourist destinations, Orlando is home to UCF’s top-5 hospitality school in the world and a hub for entertainment innovation with the world’s best game design graduate program. We also are the No. 1 supplier of graduates to the aerospace and defense industries and the nation’s top cybersecurity team thanks to strong partnership with industry leaders like Lockheed Martin and the nation’s leading research centers and companies for modeling and simulation.
UCF is one of only three public universities to improve our U.S. News & World Report overall national ranking each of the past five years. We are Florida’s highest-ranking university for innovation (No. 15), and more than 30 of our graduate programs are ranked among the nation’s best, including 10 graduate engineering rankings in the top 50 for public universities.
UCF is a destination for the nation’s top-performing students, where out commitment to successful outcomes for students of every background has never been stronger. Our Fall 2021 freshmen class had an average SAT score of 1325 and an average high school GPA of 4.25. With 357 enrolled, UCF was ninth in the nation for the number of National Merit Scholars, and we enroll and graduate more Pell-eligible students than the 12 Ivy Plus universities combined.
In everything we do, UCF is building a university for the future that empowers people and their ideas to make a difference in the world while leveraging our location and unique strengths as a public metropolitan research university nationally recognized for innovation, research and social mobility.

When Walt Disney chose Central Florida’s orange groves for his iconic theme park, he envisioned a magical kingdom that would draw millions from around the globe. More than 50 years later, that same spirit of innovation and imagination has resulted in an international destination for hospitality education and research at the University of Central Florida — repeatedly named one of the world’s top 5 hospitality schools.
Uniquely positioned in the center of international attractions, top hotels and restaurants and a massive convention center, UCF’s Rosen College of Hospitality Management offers unrivaled opportunities for globally recognized faculty to partner with industry leaders on research and prepare the next generation of talent. As the largest hospitality educator in the world, the Rosen College takes an international and interdisciplinary approach to its programs, partnerships and student experiences. That means UCF and its top-caliber researchers and exceptional students and graduates aren’t just leaders in hospitality and tourism, they are leading the future.
At UCF, hospitality is more than tourism and foodservice studies. It is an opportunity for cross-disciplinary innovation and influential industry partnerships with the world’s most-recognizable companies to inspire and build a more sustainable and compassionate future.
The first graduating class from UCF’s themed experience track in the theatre Master of Fine Arts program is prepared to create lands and parks that we can only imagine.
UCF offers a joint degree in hospitality and tourism management and medicine to improve doctors’ cultural competency and service-oriented care with its first graduate this spring.
Researchers at the Dick Pope Sr. Institute for Tourism Studies developed a sustainable tourism masterplan to make the island of Bonaire the first “blue destination” in the Caribbean.

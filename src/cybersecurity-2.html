In a world of increasing cyber attacks, not all superheroes wear capes. University of Central Florida researchers are leading the way in fortifying our infrastructure and advancing technologies to protect everything and everyone. From power grid resiliency to preventing financial fraud, UCF faculty are improving our security and way of life through a thriving cybersecurity and privacy research cluster, partnerships with Fortune 500 companies and preparing a new generation of cyber defenders to fight the surge of threats.
Meeting the urgent demand for cybersecurity talent means building the best programs and opportunities for our future workforce. From its national success in cyberdefense competitions to a new master’s program in cybersecurity and privacy, UCF is a leader in training the next generation of defenders against attacks and data breaches estimated to cost the U.S. more than $110 billion annually. Last year, the National Science Foundation awarded UCF nearly $2.9 million for scholarships to expand the talent pool protecting our nation’s cybersecurity.
Because of its stand-out faculty, alumni and reputation, UCF more than quadrupled enrollment in its nationally ranked computer science-related majors during the past decade. It also launched a minor in secure computing and networks, a master’s in digital forensics and a graduate certificate in modeling and simulation of behavioral cybersecurity to meet exploding demand.

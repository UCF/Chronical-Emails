Like the late legendary physicist, Stephen Hawking, University of Central Florida researchers are probing the cosmos to explain its greatest mysteries. UCF’s Stephen W. Hawking Center for Microgravity Research and Education — the first American research center to bear his name — builds on Hawking’s groundbreaking discoveries about how the universe formed and fulfills his wishes to advance microgravity research. The center is also exploring ways of making space travel safer and inhabiting Mars or the Moon.
Jaden Chambers, Margarita Bassil and Adolfo Alvaro Rivera are living their dreams working for NASA at Kennedy Space Center. Chambers and Rivera are interning, and Bassil turned her internship into a full-time job.
Our Rising Stars
19 UCF students — the most from any university — were selected for NASA’s coveted, prestigious intern employment program at Kennedy Space Center, where nearly one-third of employees are UCF alumni.
America’s Destination for Space Science
Just 35 miles from Kennedy Space Center, where Hawking took his famous Zero-G flight, the nation’s future rocket scientists are staking their claim to the $1 trillion-plus space economy by 2040. At UCF, faculty aren’t the only ones getting their hands dirty (with simulated moon dust or lunar concrete). Students at all levels take part, and sometimes lead, cutting-edge efforts.

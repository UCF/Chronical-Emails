When Thomas Edison first invented the light bulb, worries about power grid resiliency, climate change and cyberattacks weren’t even a glimmer. But today’s modern electric grids face many threats from humans and nature. A new microgrid control laboratory at the University of Central Florida lets researchers and future innovators test solutions to meet the rapidly rising need for more sustainable, affordable and reliable energy.
The lab, co-sponsored by Florida Power & Light and GE Digital, simulates challenges like mass outages and mimics real-life grid control operations with evolving renewable technologies. It is both a critical training ground for electrical and computer engineers and a research incubator for students and faculty from various disciplines working on new energy resources and making our power systems more resilient and secure. And it’s just the latest example of UCF’s growing powerhouse reputation in energy innovation research through industry collaboration that’s attracting the nation’s brightest talent.
Through innovation and government and industry partnerships with companies like GE, Mitsubishi Power and Siemens, UCF is reimaging tomorrow’s energy solutions to power a future that’s safer and more resilient and reliable for us all.
Interdisciplinary faculty research clusters are tackling new energy generation and sustainable energy solutions.
UCF is part of a new coalition accelerating electric transportation and addressing infrastructure challenges in the Southeast.
Researchers are leading a $10-million NASA project to develop zero-carbon jet engines.
UCF is working with the U.S. Department of Energy to make clean-burning, advanced gas turbine technologies.

When veterans return from duty or first responders are called to the scene of a building collapse, their selfless actions can take a toll on their mental health. Many of our bravest suffer from PTSD for years after an incident.
Thanks to its innovative virtual reality technology — backed by the U.S. Department of Defense and U.S. Army — UCF RESTORES, a clinic at the University of Central Florida led by Professor Deborah Beidel, is finding success in helping veterans and first-responders improve their mental health and reclaim their lives. Their results have been so effective that the clinic has expanded its treatment to also include victims of other trauma, including sexual assault and mass shootings.

We needed a vaccine, and ucf alum darin edwards gave the future of medicine so much more.
When the world needed an antidote for the coronavirus pandemic, we didn’t have a decade to spare waiting for a traditional vaccine, and UCF graduate Darin Edwards knew it. As director of immunology for Moderna, Darin also knew he had the answer and saw an opportunity for his team to impact the world. Using his expertise in vaccine development and his educational background at UCF as a three-time graduate with a doctorate in biomedical sciences, he guided Moderna’s team through the unprecedented process of creating the mRNA vaccine in 48 hours and licensing it in under a year — creating a path to fight future pandemics and opening up possibilities for the future of new treatments in medicine

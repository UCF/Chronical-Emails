When the world needed an antidote for the coronavirus pandemic, we didn’t have a decade to spare waiting for a traditional vaccine, and UCF graduate and Director of Immunology at Moderna, Darin Edwards, knew it. Darin also knew he had the answer and saw…
Check out how Edwards helped open up possibilities for the future of medicine:
Using his expertise in vaccine development and his educational background at UCF as a three-time graduate with a doctorate in biomedical sciences, he guided Moderna’s team through the unprecedented process of creating the mRNA vaccine in 48 hours and licensing it in under a year — creating a path to fight future pandemics and opening up possibilities for the future of new treatments in medicine.
“When I think about what I’m going to remember most from this last year, it’s that moment of satisfaction of knowing that my parents were going to be OK and knowing I helped protect people.”
Edwards says he looks to grow Moderna’s infectious disease team and resources to enable the development of more vaccines against more targets and extending to therapeutics, cancers and other rare diseases.

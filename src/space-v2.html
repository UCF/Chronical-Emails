Established in 1963 to provide talent to the burgeoning space industry at nearby Kennedy Space Center, the University of Central Florida (UCF) has a legacy of providing a talent pipeline to help NASA reach its goals. Today, our alums are helping to lead the logistics and ensure NASA returns to the moon via Gateway — a new lunar orbiting outpost is crucial to the new era of space exploration.
DSL managers Jenny Lyons and Mark Wiese — and 18 other Knights — credit their studies at UCF for helping them develop the skills needed to collaborate with a team of scientists, engineering, and business experts to develop Gateway’s innovative technology. With nearly one-third of Kennedy Space Center employees being alumni, UCF is dedicated to training the next generation of experts needed to advance deep space exploration.
